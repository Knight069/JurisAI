Features

E-discovery

AI can be used to identify and review electronic data that is relevant to a legal case. This can help lawyers quickly and efficiently find the evidence they need. AI can also be used to deduplicate data and to tag data with metadata.

Draft Generation

AI can be used to create first drafts of legal documents, such as contracts, wills, and trusts. This can save lawyers time and allow them to focus on more complex tasks. AI can also be used to generate legal documents that are tailored to the specific needs of the client.

Contract/Document Review

AI can be used to review large amounts of documents, such as emails and financial records. This can help lawyers identify relevant documents and reduce the time spent on document review. AI can also be used to flag documents that may be important to the case.

Deposition

AI can be used to create transcripts of depositions, which are sworn statements taken from witnesses in a legal case. This can save lawyers time and allow them to focus on other aspects of the case. AI can also be used to identify key points in depositions and to generate summaries of the depositions.

AI Assistant

AI can be used to create virtual legal assistants that can help lawyers with tasks such as scheduling appointments and managing email. This can free up lawyers to focus on more important tasks. AI can also be used to generate reports that summarize the tasks that a virtual assistant has completed.

Learn

AI can be used for training and education in the legal industry. This can help lawyers learn new skills and stay up-to-date on the latest legal developments. AI can also be used to generate reports that summarize the training that a lawyer has completed.
